# Energy System Management Portfolio - Day 2

## Project: Generator Fuel Economics Analysis

### Modeling Backup Power Operational Costs in Current Nigerian Market

## Executive Summary

Developed a comprehensive economic model analyzing the true cost of backup power generation using an 11kVA petrol generator at current fuel prices (₦900/L). The analysis provides data-driven insights into operational expenses, efficiency factors, and financial implications for households relying on generators during grid outages.

## Technical Challenge

Translate household energy demand (from Day 1) into:

1. Fuel consumption based on generator efficiency curves
2. Operational costs including maintenance and capital depreciation
3. Cost per kWh comparisons with grid electricity
4. Sensitivity analysis for fuel price volatility

## Key Findings

### Financial Impact

- **Daily Operational Cost**: ₦15,000-₦18,000 for 24-hour operation
- **Monthly Expenditure**: ₦450,000-₦540,000
- **Annual Budget Impact**: ₦5.4M-₦6.5M
- **Cost per kWh**: ₦180-₦220 (60-100% premium over grid)

### Operational Insights

- **Generator Efficiency**: 60-78% depending on load factor
- **Optimal Operation**: 50-75% of rated capacity
- **Fuel Consumption**: 40-65 liters daily for average household
- **Environmental Impact**: 15-20 tons CO2 annually

### Risk Analysis

- **Fuel Price Sensitivity**: 25% monthly volatility significantly impacts costs
- **Maintenance Requirements**: Adds 20% to operational expenses
- **Equipment Lifespan**: Reduced to 3 years with continuous operation
